Airports

  • Madison - Dane County Regional Airport (MSN)

    The closest and simplest airport is the Dane County Regional Airport (MSN). MSN is only a 15 drive to Madison downtown - the airport has rental cars available, multiple taxi services are available for pickup, and some hotels provide courtesy shuttles for guests. Unfortunately, MSN is a smaller airport. Madison may not have direct flights to your airport, and it can be expensive to fly in.

    Map

  • Milwaukee - General Mitchell International Airport (MKE)

    The second closest airport is near downtown Milwaukee, MKE. MKE is a 1 hour and 30 minutes drive from Madison downtown. Rental cars are available, and there is also a Greyhound route to Madison. While larger than MSN, MKE still may not have direct flights to all cities.

    Map

  • Chicago - O'Hare Airport (ORD)

    If you know someone or care to make a stop in Chicago, ORD is a 2 hour drive from Madison downtown. In addition to Rental cars, there are a few bus services (Megabus, Greyhound) that have routes to Madison. ORD has direct lines to most airports. Since it's the largest airport nearby, flights are typically cheaper with more options.

Driving Routes

  • From the East

    I-80/I-90 West is the main route to Madison. The roads do include tolls - the drive is much easier with an I-Pass or EZPass. Typically traffic is at its worst around Chicago, so check for route options that keep farther south in Illinois.


  • From the South

    Unless you intend to stop in Chicago, if you are able to avoid the city you will avoid a majority of traffic. Taking I-39 North to Rockford could cut a large amount of time.


  • From the West

    We have driven a number of routes from the West, and have found they tend to be fairly equal. If you intend to stop along the way, we have recommendations in particular for Minneapolis, MN; Des Moines, IA; and Cedar Rapids IA.


  • From the North

    Enjoy the view, and wave to Canada on your way past!
